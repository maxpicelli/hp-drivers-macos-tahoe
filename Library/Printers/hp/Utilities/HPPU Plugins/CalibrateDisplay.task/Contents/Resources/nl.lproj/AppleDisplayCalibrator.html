How to calibrate your display using the Apple Display Calibrator Assistant
 
Monitors exhibit color degradation over time. The ICC profile that comes with some monitors gradually becomes invalid, and color accuracy decreases. Use the Apple Display Calibrator periodically to produce a custom ICC profile and to ensure optimal color performance. While the period of calibration interval varies between monitors, professionals generally recommend calibrating a monitor every two weeks.
 
To open the Display Calibrator Assistant:
 
1. Open System Preferences and click Displays.
 
2. Click Color.
 
3. Click Calibrate. The Display Calibrator Assistant walks you through calibrating your display. To obtain a more detailed display calibration, select the Expert Mode checkbox.
