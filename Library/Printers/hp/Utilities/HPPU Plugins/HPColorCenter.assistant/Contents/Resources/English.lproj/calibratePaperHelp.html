Step 1 - Calibrate
Printer calibration is needed to produce repeatable color for your printer, ink and paper combination. It works by linearizing your printer and determining the maximum achievable density for each of the inks. The process consists of printing and measuring patches of different densities of each ink individually.

Step 2 - Finished
Once calibration is complete, you can browse the list of all papers your printer 'knows' about. For each paper, it lists the paper category or custom paper name, the calibration status, and the calibration date. If the calibration status of a certain paper reads "OK", that means that the printer has recently been calibrated for this paper, which is ready for use. If the status reads "Obsolete", that means that a calibration has been performed at least once, but it is not reliable anymore because too much time has passed, a printhead has been replaced or any other condition has changed. You should re-calibrate the printer for this paper to ensure best results. If the status reads "Recommended", that means that no calibration has been performed yet. You should calibrate the printer for this paper to ensure best results.As well, in papers that support gloss enhancer, decide to enable or disable it.
